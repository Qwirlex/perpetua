# Perpetua, site walkthrough video script

A simple three minute screen recording of the live site at https://tradeperpetua.xyz.
Plain language, no jargon, anyone can follow it. Record your screen, open the site, and
say each line in your own words while you show the part in brackets.

If you would rather speak Russian, say it in Russian and add English subtitles. The lines
below are written plain on purpose, keep them simple when you talk.

Total time, about three minutes. Read at a calm pace.

---

## 0:00 to 0:25, the opening

**[SHOW: open https://tradeperpetua.xyz, the top of the page, the title on screen]**

Say:
> This is Perpetua. It is an AI agent that pays for its own work. Normally an AI costs
> you money every time it runs, and it stops the moment you stop paying. Perpetua is
> different. It earns its own money and keeps itself running. Let me show you how, live,
> on this page.

## 0:25 to 1:00, the balance that grows by itself

**[SHOW: the four cards, then the balance chart climbing]**

Say:
> Look at these numbers. The agent started with about two cents. That is the only money
> anyone ever gave it. After that, nobody adds a single cent.
>
> Now look at this line. It is the agent's balance over time, and it keeps going up. The
> agent is making more than it spends, all on its own. Up here it says total earned, and
> here total spent. Earned is bigger than spent, so it stays alive and grows.

## 1:00 to 1:45, what it actually sells

**[SHOW: scroll down to the Signal feed on the left]**

Say:
> So how does it earn? It studies the crypto market and writes a short report on the
> risk. You can see them here. Each one says how risky things look right now, a score out
> of a hundred, and whether the price is going up or down, with a simple explanation.
>
> These reports are the product. The agent makes them, and other software agents buy them.
> Every report it writes is something it can sell, and the money it earns pays for writing
> the next one. That is the whole loop. It learns, it sells what it learned, it uses the
> money to learn more.

## 1:45 to 2:30, the proof, real money on a real blockchain

**[SHOW: the Ledger on the right, point at a Signal sale row, then click the "on chain" link]**

Say:
> And this is not pretend. On the right is the ledger, every sale and every cost. Each
> sale is a real payment in a digital dollar called USDC, on a real blockchain.
>
> Watch, I click this on chain link.

**[SHOW: the BaseScan page opens, the transaction with Status Success]**

> This is the real transaction. It says Success. It shows zero point zero one USDC moving
> from a buyer to the agent, on the Base network. So the money is real and you can check
> every single sale yourself.

## 2:30 to 3:00, the big idea and the close

**[SHOW: go back to the site, the climbing chart again]**

Say:
> Here is why this matters. Once an agent can pay its own way, it does not need anyone to
> fund it. It can keep working for as long as it wants, because it earns more than it
> costs. You could run thousands of these, each paying for itself.
>
> That is Perpetua. An AI that funds its own future by selling what it learns. Thanks for
> watching.

---

## Recording tips

- Open the site full screen so the chart is big and clear.
- Before you start, the chart climbs slowly. If you want it to visibly move during the
  clip, ask me to speed the cycle up for a few minutes while you record.
- Speak slowly and calmly. The idea is strong, it does not need a loud voice.
- The single best moment is the climbing balance chart and the real BaseScan transaction.
  Give both a few seconds on screen.
